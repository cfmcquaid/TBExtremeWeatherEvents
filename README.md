**The effects of extreme weather events on tuberculosis: a living systematic review**

**Background:**
Extreme weather events likely pose significant but under-recognised challenges for the global tuberculosis epidemic. As the frequency, intensity, and duration of these events increases due to climate change, understanding their effects on tuberculosis is essential. We systematically reviewed the literature for evidence on how extreme weather events affect tuberculosis.

**Methods:**
We searched PubMed/MEDLINE, Web of Science, Embase, Global Health, and Environment Complete/Green File from database inception to November 2025, supplemented by grey literature searches, for studies investigating the effects of extreme weather events on tuberculosis outcomes. Two reviewers independently screened studies and extracted data. We synthesised findings narratively and developed a conceptual framework using systems thinking and concept mapping.  

**Findings:**
We found 28 studies from 39 countries describing the effects of storms, droughts, floods, wildfires, and dust storms on a range of tuberculosis outcomes. Evidence was limited in both quality and breadth - studies were concentrated in the Americas and on impacts of storms, and a limited number used rigorous methodologies. Our evidence synthesis showed how extreme weather events can affect tuberculosis through two main pathways: health system disruptions (e.g. infrastructure damage, workforce displacement, supply chain challenges), interrupting the provision of tuberculosis prevention, diagnosis, and care; and social and health determinants (e.g. changes to living conditions, food insecurity, mental ill health), increasing risk of tuberculosis transmission, progression, and poor outcomes. Effects varied substantially by event type and context. While no studies formally evaluated interventions to mitigate effects, several documented potential interventions to build climate resilience at individual, health system, and community levels. 
This repository contains a summary table of results, and will be updated every 6 month for the next 5 years.

**Interpretation:**
Extreme weather events can affect tuberculosis through diverse, interrelated pathways. Further research is urgently needed to characterise causal relationships and quantify effects. As these events intensify, building climate-resilient tuberculosis programmes and mitigating underlying climate drivers are essential to prevent climate change from undermining efforts to end the global tuberculosis epidemic.
